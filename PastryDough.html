Julia Child’s Pastry Dough

From Nelson’s Kitchen
Print

From The Way to Cook by Julia Child

This is a master recipe. The details are important. The most important thing is to not overwork this dough and not add too much water. The dough will just come together and you will still see small pieces of butter in it.

Ingredients:
 
  • 1 1/2 cups unbleached ﬂour
  • 1/2 cup cake ﬂour
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 6 ounces cold cultured unsalted butter (12 tbs or 1 1/2 sticks)
  • 4 tablespoons vegetable shortening or leaf lard (I like to use lard)
  • 1/2 cup ice water
Procedure:
 
  1. Cut the Butter
    • Using a paring knife, cut the cold butter into 1/2 inch cubes.
  2. Food Processor Method
    • Add the ﬂour and salt pulse once to mix.
    • Add the butter pulse 5 or 6 times to break up the butter.
    • Add the shortening or lard and pulse until it resembles crumbs.
    • Add the ice water pulsing until you have a cohesive dough, it should hold together when you press it in your hand. Do not overwork the dough.
  3. Pastry Cutter Method
    • Add the ﬂour and salt and stir with a fork.
    • Add the butter and break it up with your pastry cutter, a fork, or the tip of your ﬁngers.
    • Add the shortening or lard and mix it until it resembles crumbs. Yu should still see little pieces of butter in the dough.
    • Add the water a little at a time until you have a cohesive dough that holds together when pressed in your hand.
  4. Form and Wrap the Dough
    • Dump out onto work surface and form into a large square (or two discs if you will use for pies).
    • Wrap in plastic and refrigerate at least 30 minutes but one hour is preferable.
    • The dough can be kept refrigerated for several days.
